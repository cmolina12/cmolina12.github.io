ABOUT

My name is Camilo Molina. I'm from Barranquilla, Colombia and currently based in Bogotá, where I’m finishing a double major in Industrial Engineering and Systems and Computing Engineering at Universidad de los Andes. I’m passionate about optimization, programming, and designing systems that make complex problems manageable and solvable.

My work blends algorithmic thinking with full-stack development and applied research. I’ve built tools like Flowplan, a course scheduling optimizer, and developed a structured Obsidian vault to manage my knowledge, track habits, and streamline personal workflows. I enjoy bridging the gap between technical precision and practical impact — from backend services in Java with Spring Boot to clean and responsive frontend design.

Outside of academics and engineering, I care deeply about digital privacy and autonomy. I actively explore topics like anti-fingerprinting, data minimization, and operational security (OPSEC) — using hardened browsers, decentralized tools, and privacy-first workflows to better understand and control the digital footprint we leave behind.

Whether you're working on something technical, care about digital rights, or want to build something impactful — I'm always open to collaborating, exchanging ideas, or just having a meaningful conversation.
